About Nick

One Life, Many Chapters

I wrote Sawyer's Reach to raise a conversation — about AI, about technology, about what we're building and who it's really for. The setting, the characters, the community at the heart of the story — I based all of it on the people and places I love most. My family is my world.

I live in Batavia, Illinois and teach 6th grade math at Indian Creek Middle School in Waterman — about 40 minutes away through some of the most beautiful country roads I've ever driven. I spent 22+ years in a large high-poverty, high-ESL district near Chicago, and worked as an educational consultant mainly via my YouTube channel and direct outreach. Moving to Indian Creek was a deliberate choice.

My morning drive through small towns, forests, and open farmland became a time for self-reflection and growth. People wave to each other on the road out here. I've healed and grown in ways I didn't expect. And I finally had the mental space to write.

I got into building and flying drones during COVID while teaching from home — and I've been hooked ever since. I'm also a licensed ham radio operator, an FAA-certified commercial drone pilot, and someone who has always loved seeing how things work. I hold a Master of Science in Education.

My wife April is a professional photographer. Our three sons are the reason everything I do matters.

Beyond the Classroom

Life Outside School

Teaching is the through-line — but it's not all I am. These are the other things that fill my days, and that kept finding their way into Sawyer's Reach whether I planned them there or not.

📻

Ham Radio & Mesh Networking

Licensed amateur radio operator with a particular interest in Meshtastic and LoRa mesh networking — building off-grid community communication networks from scratch, no internet required. The mesh network in Sawyer's Reach is based on this real technology.

🚁

Building & Flying Drones

I got into drones during COVID while teaching from home — and I've been hooked ever since. FAA Part 107 certified. KAELA arriving on a drone wasn't a random choice.

🌲

The Outdoors

Camping, hiking, fishing. My 40-minute drive through country roads, forests, and small towns to Indian Creek Middle School every morning has become one of the best parts of my day. People wave to each other on the road out here.

🔧

Making & Fixing Things

I've always loved seeing how things work — and then making them work better, or fixing them when they don't. Electronics, mechanics, whatever the project calls for. Fix it first, replace it last.
