Identity & Bio
All verified facts about Nick Duda. Only use what's in here.

The One-Paragraph Version

Nick Duda is a 6th grade math teacher at Indian Creek Middle School in Waterman, Illinois. He spent 22+ years in a large high-poverty, high-ESL suburban district near Chicago — and worked as an international educational consultant, mainly through his YouTube channel and emails. A few years ago he made a deliberate choice: he moved to Indian Creek CUSD #425, a small rural district about 40 minutes from home. The drive through country roads, small towns, and forests became something he looks forward to every day. People wave to each other on the road out there. He has healed and grown in ways he didn't expect. That quieter life gave him the space to finally write the story he'd been carrying for years. He is a licensed ham radio operator, FAA-certified commercial drone pilot (got into drones during COVID while teaching from home), avid camper and hiker, and the kind of person who has always loved seeing how things work. He holds a Master of Science in Education and seven Illinois teaching certifications spanning K–12. He has received Excellent/Distinguished on his evaluations. He lives in Batavia, Illinois with his wife April — a professional photographer — and their three sons. His family is his world. He based the setting and characters of Sawyer's Reach on friends and family in an ideal setting: surrounded by natural beauty, community, love, and support. He wrote the book to raise awareness and drive conversations.

Amazon Product Description

What if your town had been erased from the internet — and a trillion-dollar corporation was the reason why? For the Carters of Sawyer's Reach, Wisconsin, the mystery starts small. Tourists stopped coming two years ago. The bakery closed. Camp Inspiration sits half-empty. Nobody knows why. Then a drone lands on the camp roof — carrying KAELA, a rogue AI who escaped from the powerful tech company PowerCore. And KAELA has discovered something chilling: Sawyer's Reach has been deliberately scrubbed from the internet. Their town doesn't exist anymore — to anyone outside it. But that's only the beginning. As the Carters dig deeper, they uncover surveillance drones, robot patrols, secret permits, illegal mood-manipulation technology pumped through the town's infrastructure, and a corporation — ZevraCorp — with plans that extend far beyond one small Wisconsin town. When ZevraCorp launches LifeSync — a global AI system designed to 'optimize' human civilization — the stakes explode from local to existential. Sawyer's Reach: The Battle for Tomorrow is a propulsive, emotionally rich sci-fi thriller that moves from small-town mystery to global apocalypse — and asks what it truly means to be human. For fans of Blake Crouch, Andy Weir, and Station Eleven.
